How supercapacitors, lithium-ion batteries, and gasoline compare



Supercapacitors, gasoline, and lithium-ion batteries use remarkably different energy storage mechanisms. Let's see how they stack up.



Energy storage device Specific energy (MJ/kg) Energy density (MJ/liter) Power density (W/kg) Environmental considerations
Lithium-ion batteries 0.72 0.9-2.23 250-340 Lithium is non-renewable and hard to recycle.
Gasoline 47.2 34 Thermodynamically limitied Gasoline contributes to increasing atmosphere CO2 and is increasingly limited supply.
Supercapacitors 0.1 0.1 2500-25000 Several, but not all, supercapacitors use lithium and other elements that are harmful.
Graphene supercapacitors 0.56 ~.1 Current methods of mass production use copious amounts of strong acids.


Some of this data is very surprising. Gasoline is awesome at energy storage - it stores fifty times more energy per kilogram than lithium-ion batteries, let alone supercapacitors. Power density is also interesting. It doesn't really make sense to talk about gasoline's power density because different engines release gasoline's energy at different rates, but a supercapacitor can have up to a hundred times higher power density than a battery. This means that they can dump energy or take in energy that much faster. That's extremely useful for many applications - keep reading to learn more.
