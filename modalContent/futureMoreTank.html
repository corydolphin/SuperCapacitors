Nanotube supercapacitors

Supercapacitors are so great because they have a very high surface area, and a team at MIT has figured out how to even further increase the surface area: carbon nanotubes. These nanotubes cling to the surface of the capacitor, making a thick mat of surface for charge to build on. These might increase capacitance by hundreds! Professor Joel Schindall believes that these could get the energy density of a supercapacitor improved to be about half of that of a lithium-ion cell - making them comparable. He estimates approximately 5 years until commercialization.
